CRISPR: An unprecedented
genetic breakthrough

by Juan Camilo Veloza published on october 31, 2024

CRISPR technology makes it possible to modify the genome in a targeted and precise way. This innovative genetic advance could transform human evolution, raising ethical questions about human nature and the control of our biology.

CRISPR

CRISPR, or “Clustered Regularity Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats”, is a revolutionary technology that enables genetic material of living organisms. To better understand this CRIPRs can be thought of as “molecular scissors” capable of cutting and locating specific DNA segments. This modification could make it possible to correct genetic mutations responsible for disease, make agricultural crops more resistant, or even transform animal species.


This genetic innovation, developed in the 2010s, represents a major evolution in our biology. It used in many fields, such as medicine and agriculture, and could influence the future of human the future of human evolution. However, while promising, this technology also raises ethical questions about the control we exert over human nature and the potential dangers associated with it.

The machine and the ethics of the future

The CRISPR tool could mark a decisive step in evolution, integrating technology and biology in an unprecedented way. Some researchers and thinkers see the ability to modify the genome as a way of improving human physical and cognitive capacities, a concept reminiscent of transhumanist ideals.


This would imply that, thanks to biotechnology, man could approach a “human machine”, in which his biological characteristics would be optimized or enhanced through genetic and technological modification. Optimized or enhanced through genetic and technological modification.p>

However, this rapprochement between biology and machine raises important ethical questions. Modifying human nature nature in order to “improve” certain abilities raises the question of control. Who will decide what constitutes an improvement? How far should we push these modifications? And above all, who will have access to these innovations? advances risk creating a two-tier society, where those who can afford these technologies will set those who can't afford them, exacerbating social inequalities.


Pour mieux comprendre ces enjeux, nous allons voir le livre Transhumanisme de Christian Byk qui apporte des pistes de réflexion essentielles. Byk interroge la manière dont la technologie pourrait transformer non seulement nos corps, mais aussi nos valeurs, nos sociétés et notre vision de l’humanité. Il souligne la nécessité d’établir extrêmes ou à une déshumanisation progressive de l’espèce humaine
