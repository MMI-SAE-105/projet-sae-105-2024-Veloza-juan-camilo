Lost in the scientific vocabulary of genetic modification?

Don't worry, here's a list of words that can be difficult to understand due to their complexity.

Transhumanism

Transhumanism, posthumanism or human enhancement refers to an intellectual movement whose ambition is the transformation or improvement of man through the means of science and technology. Rejecting the idea that the human species is inalterable, transhumanists argue that we need to boost and realize our inherent potential, to improve our human characteristics, and even to eliminate certain aspects of the human condition deemed negative: suffering, aging, disease, cognitive deficits, senility, imperfections of all kinds.


Génome

The genome is the set of DNA instructions present in a cell. In humans, the genome consists of 23 pairs of chromosomes in the cell nucleus, plus a small chromosome in the cell mitochondria.


Escherichia Coli

Is a bacterium found in the intestinal microbiota of humans and animals. Although most strains of Escherichiacoli are harmless, some, like EHEC, have acquired virulence factors and are therefore pathogenic.


Palindromic sequence

A palindrome is a segment of DNA or RNA that is identical when read in opposite directions on the two strands of the double helix.


Streptococcus thermophilus bacteria

Streptococcus thermophilus is a bacterium that forms part of the intestinal microbiota.


RNA

Ribonucleic acid (RNA) is an acid found in all living cells that shares structural similarities with DNA. However, unlike DNA, RNA is more often made up of a single strand of RNA.p>

Protéine Cas9

The Cas9 protein is a guide RNA-directed DNA enzyme associated with the CRISPR system.
